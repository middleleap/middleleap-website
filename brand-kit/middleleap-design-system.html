Rules

  • Clearspace: keep a margin equal to the square's width on all sides of the lockup.
  • Minimum sizes: full lockup 120px wide; mark alone 20px. Below 20px use the diamond only.
  • The diamond is always ember, always 45°. Never recolour, never rotate to any other angle.
  • The wordmark never breaks across lines and never adds a space between Middle and Leap.
  • On photography, place the lockup on an ink scrim (rgba 8,8,8 at 70%+) — never directly on imagery.

The site's UI is sharp: buttons, inputs, cards and tables sit at 0px, matching middleleap.com. Rounded corners belong to the mark alone — the pivot glyphs (logo, checkbox, toggle thumb, loader) keep their 16% squircle geometry, which is what makes them read as the brand and not as UI. Elevation stays sparing: shadow-1 raised, shadow-2 overlays, shadow-ember for the primary CTA hover only.

Iconography

24px grid, 1.75px stroke, rounded caps and joins. Where an icon needs a point of emphasis — a dot, a node, a marker — it uses the pivot diamond, never a circle.

08

Voice

MiddleLeap talks like a senior operator, not an agency. Precise, direct, in the language of mandates and execution — confident enough to be plain.

SAY

"From strategic mandate to market execution."

Concrete, active, names the journey. Verbs carry the sentence; claims are specific enough to be testable.

NOT

"Unlocking synergies through best-in-class digital transformation journeys."

Abstract nouns stacked on abstract nouns. If a competitor could say it, it isn't ours.

  • Active voice, present tense. "We design the operating model" — not "operating models are designed".
  • Regulatory language stays exact: standards, licence classes and framework names are never paraphrased or loosely capitalised.
  • Buttons say what happens: "Discuss a mandate", "Start a sprint" — never "Submit", "Learn more", "Get started".
  • Numbers over adjectives. "First to certify" beats "market-leading". If we can't count it, we don't claim it.
  • Errors state the fault and the fix, in one breath. No apologies, no vagueness.
