## The 15% ceiling

Most engineering organizations have adopted AI coding assistants and seen a
modest, real productivity bump — and then plateaued. The gains stall around
15% because the work was reorganized around the tool, not the other way around.

## Why bolting on fails

- AI accelerates keystrokes, but keystrokes were never the constraint.
- The real bottleneck is the decision-and-review loop, which stays human-paced.
- Process designed for human throughput caps agent throughput.

## The agent-factory model

- From individual copilots to coordinated agent fleets.
- Specification-first delivery as the unit of work.
- The knowledge flywheel: every project compounds the next.

## What changes for the CTO

- Org design: fewer, more senior decision-makers directing agent fleets.
- Governance moves left, encoded as policy-as-code.
- Measuring outcomes, not commits.
